How It Works

From Problem to Resolution

The JusticeLK ecosystem guides a citizen from their first moment of need to a qualified legal professional — in minutes, not months.

1
Citizen Identifies Their Problem
A villager, child, foreigner or professional faces a legal issue — land dispute, abuse, wage theft, visa problem, or any legal matter.
2
Access the Right Platform
They access JusticeLK (with a field officer), JusticeKids (children), LexLK (professional help), or JusticeSL (foreign nationals) — all free, in their language.
3
Case Registered & AI-Summarised
The field officer registers the case through a guided 7-step wizard. AI generates a professional case summary and court petition instantly.
4
Automatically Routed to Legal Aid
The case file — including all documents — is automatically sent to the Legal Aid Commission or a registered private attorney based on the citizen's means.
5
Client Tracks Progress by Token
The citizen receives a unique token number. They can track their case status at any time by entering the token — no login required.

Our Mission & Proposal

Why Sri Lanka
Needs This

The gap between Sri Lanka's legal system and its citizens — particularly in rural areas — is one of the most persistent challenges to equal justice under the law.

The Problem We Solve
Language barrier — Most legal documents and court proceedings are in Sinhala, leaving Tamil-speaking communities and foreign nationals without access.
Geographic isolation — Rural citizens in remote districts cannot travel to Colombo or district capitals to access legal services.
Child vulnerability — Children facing abuse have no accessible, anonymous channel to seek help — leading to unreported cases.
Legal illiteracy — The majority of citizens are unaware of their basic rights, making them vulnerable to exploitation and injustice.
Cost barrier — Private legal fees are unaffordable for low-income families, and the Legal Aid Commission is under-utilised due to lack of awareness.
What JusticeLK Delivers
Fully operational today — All four platforms are live, tested and accessible at no cost to any user.
All 25 districts — Field officer case management covers every district in Sri Lanka, including conflict-affected areas.
Trilingual — All platforms operate in Sinhala, Tamil and English. LexLK extends to 10 additional languages.
Works offline — JusticeLK is a Progressive Web App that works fully in remote villages without internet connectivity.
Child-safe design — JusticeKids is built to international child safety standards with anonymous SOS, no personal data collection, and no advertising.
30+ languages — All platforms operate with Google Translate integration covering over 30 languages, with native Sinhala, Tamil and English interfaces, making the ecosystem accessible to every community in Sri Lanka and internationally.
🆘 Special Feature
Integrated Emergency SOS System

The Justice LK Ecosystem incorporates an advanced, cross-platform SOS emergency response system — a nationally significant safety infrastructure that operates across all four platforms and is designed for integration with official emergency services.

📍
Live GPS Location
One press sends precise GPS coordinates to the safety response team and integrated emergency services.
🦁
Child-Specific SOS
JusticeKids anonymous SOS routes directly to NCPA (1929). No child needs to identify themselves to receive help.
🌐
Foreign National SOS
JusticeSL SOS connects tourists and expatriates with Tourist Police (011 269 1777) and multilingual emergency support.
🏛️
Official Integration Ready
Designed for direct integration with Police 119, NCPA 1929, Ambulance 1990, and Women & Children's Bureau (1938).

All SOS location data is encrypted, used solely for emergency dispatch, and not stored permanently. The system is designed to meet national emergency services integration standards.

Expanded Programme Vision

The Justice LK Ecosystem is more than a digital platform. It is a national justice access programme with a growing community, training framework, and international ambition.

🦁
JusticeKids — Education Integration
JusticeKids is being expanded into a full national legal and human rights syllabus for the Sri Lankan education system:

→ Workshops and training programmes in schools across all 25 districts
→ Student legal rights communities and committees
→ Junior legal advocates programme — training the next generation of lawyers with real attorneys and officials
→ Dedicated teacher training and certification programme
→ Parent information sessions on child rights and protection
→ Ministry of Education approved curriculum modules
→ Integration with school legal aid referral pathways
🌐
JusticeSL — Community Representative Programme
JusticeSL will train and deploy certified community representatives as the front line of the ecosystem:

→ Workshops for public representatives (Pradeshiya Sabha, Municipal Council members)
→ Training to resolve minor legal disputes without escalation
→ Rights, law, and policy training — preventing misuse of legal process
→ Specialist training: non-resident issues, children's rights, women's rights
→ Certified representatives become Chief Field Officers registered to operate JusticeLK
→ Emergency response training for all representatives
→ Community building across all 25 districts
⚖️
JusticeLK — Field Officer Certification
Appointed community representatives will be certified and registered as Chief Field Officers:

→ Full training on JusticeLK case management system
→ Legal aid routing procedures and Legal Aid Commission protocols
→ Case documentation and evidence handling
→ Attorney-client privilege and confidentiality obligations
→ Certification issued by Ace Dynamics Trading / Justice LK
→ Ongoing continuing education and platform updates
→ Direct integration with Legal Aid Commission officer network
🌏
International Expansion
The Justice LK Ecosystem is designed as a cost-effective, internationally deployable justice access solution:

→ Lightweight infrastructure — deployable in any country using GitHub Pages or equivalent
→ 30+ language support via Google Translate with native interface in key languages
→ PWA offline capability — critical for low-connectivity regions globally
→ Modular architecture — individual platforms or the full ecosystem can be localised
→ Social enterprise licensing model — sustainable without dependence on advertising
→ Aligns with UNDP SDG 16 (Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions) globally
