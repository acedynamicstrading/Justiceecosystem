How It Works

From Community
to Court

JusticeLK acts as a grassroots digital front-end — helping rural communities submit clean, structured complaints, choose free or paid legal help, and connect to the right party or the nearest District Court.

1
Person Identifies Their Problem
A villager, family or individual — particularly in rural or remote areas — has a complaint or dispute they need to bring forward through official channels.
2
Complaint Digitised via Guided Process
A field-level facilitator helps the person complete a simple guided form — available online or fully offline as a PWA. The complaint is digitised in a clean, standardised format in Sinhala, Tamil or English.
3
Early Mediation Attempted
The facilitator first reviews the complaint and attempts early resolution or mediation where possible — reducing unnecessary escalation to formal proceedings.
4
Free or Paid Legal Help — User's Choice
The system asks the user how they wish to proceed. They are free to choose any path that suits them:

Free legal help — guided to the Legal Aid Commission.
Paid — JusticeLK attorney — connected to a JusticeLK-registered private attorney via LexLK, with case documents ready.
Own attorney — the user may proceed with any attorney of their own choosing. JusticeLK-registered attorneys are not mandatory. The user has full rights to continue whichever way they prefer.
5
Structured Ticket Forwarded to District Court
The system simultaneously generates a structured digital ticket with relevant documents in a suitable format and forwards it to the nearest District Court for official processing.
6
Complainant Continues via Official Channels
The complainant proceeds through standard court procedures, supported by their chosen legal representative. JusticeLK tracks their submission by token — it does not perform any court functions.

Our Mission & Proposal

Why Sri Lanka
Needs This

The gap between Sri Lanka's legal system and its people — particularly in rural areas — is one of the most persistent challenges to equal justice under the law.

The Problem We Solve
Language barrier — Most legal documents and court proceedings are in Sinhala, leaving Tamil-speaking communities and foreign nationals without access.
Geographic isolation — Rural citizens in remote districts cannot travel to Colombo or district capitals to access legal services.
Child vulnerability — Children facing abuse have no accessible, anonymous channel to seek help — leading to unreported cases.
Legal illiteracy — The majority of people are unaware of their basic rights, making them vulnerable to exploitation and injustice.
Cost barrier — Private legal fees are unaffordable for low-income families, and the Legal Aid Commission is under-utilised due to lack of awareness.
What JusticeLK Delivers
Grassroots digital front-end — Helps rural communities digitise complaints through a simple guided process, supplying clean standardised data directly to the nearest District Court.
Facilitator-led mediation first — A field-level facilitator attempts early resolution before any formal escalation, reducing unnecessary court burden.
Complementary, not competing — JusticeLK does not replace or duplicate the Judiciary's e-Court / e-CMS project or any official digital initiative. It feeds better-organised inputs into those systems.
Fully offline PWA — Works without internet connectivity in the most remote areas, ensuring no community is left behind.
Trilingual — Sinhala, Tamil and English with Google Translate integration covering 30+ languages.
JURE & Legal Aid Dialogue 2026 aligned — This model directly supports the goals of improving access to justice outside Colombo and rural digitalization of justice inputs.
📌 Our Position
A Complementary Grassroots Front-End — Not a Replacement

JusticeLK does not replace, duplicate, or interfere with any official digital court systems. It exists to improve the quality and organisation of inputs entering those systems — particularly from rural and remote communities.

✓ What JusticeLK Is
→ A citizen-friendly digital complaint entry point
→ A grassroots PWA accessible offline in remote areas
→ A facilitator-assisted early mediation tool
→ A routing system — user chooses free (Legal Aid Commission), a JusticeLK-registered attorney (via LexLK), or their own attorney of choice — all options are valid, JusticeLK attorneys are not mandatory
→ A structured data supplier to District Courts
→ A front-end that generates well-organised, standardised documents
→ Complementary to the Judiciary's e-Court / e-CMS project
→ Aligned with JURE project goals and Legal Aid Dialogue 2026
✕ What JusticeLK Is Not
→ Not a court system or court function
→ Does not issue certified copies or court documents
→ Does not duplicate or compete with e-Court / e-CMS
→ Does not perform functions of the Judicial Service Commission
→ Does not perform functions of the Legal Aid Commission
→ Does not replace standard court procedures
→ Does not constitute legal advice

This complementary approach helps accelerate the reach of all official digitalization efforts by enabling better-organised inputs from rural communities right from the start. We believe this model aligns well with the goals of the JURE project and the Legal Aid Dialogue 2026, particularly in improving access to justice outside Colombo.

Designed for integration with Police 119, NCPA 1929, Ambulance 1990, and Women & Children's Bureau (1938).

All SOS location data is encrypted, used solely for emergency dispatch, and not stored permanently.
