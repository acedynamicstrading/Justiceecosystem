Section 5

Children's Privacy (JusticeKids)

Child Safety — Our Strongest Commitment
JusticeKids is designed from the ground up to collect zero personal data from children. No account creation is required. No names, addresses, NICs, school names, or any other identifying information is requested or stored. Children can use all features of JusticeKids anonymously.

The following child safety principles govern JusticeKids absolutely:

  • No registration required — Children access all rights education content, Leo's story, the quiz, and Ask Leo without creating any account.
  • No data storage of quiz results — Quiz answers and scores are processed locally in the browser and are not transmitted to or stored on any server.
  • No advertising — JusticeKids contains no advertising of any kind. No advertising networks have access to the platform.
  • Certificate privacy — When a child enters their name to generate a certificate, this is processed entirely in the browser. The name is never transmitted to any server.
  • Ask Leo — session only — Conversations with the Ask Leo chatbot are not stored after the browser session ends. No conversation history is retained.
  • UNCRC alignment — JusticeKids is designed in accordance with the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child, Article 16 (right to privacy).
  • Anonymous SOS — The Minor Protection Portal and SOS button do not require a child to identify themselves to receive help.

JusticeKids complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection principles applicable in Sri Lanka and internationally. If you have concerns about a child's use of the platform, contact us at the details in Section 15.

Section 6

SOS & Emergency Location Data

The SOS emergency system is available on JusticeKids, JusticeSL, and JusticeLK. When a user activates the SOS button:

  • The browser requests permission to access the device's GPS location. This permission is always explicitly requested from the user and is never granted without active consent.
  • If permission is granted, the GPS coordinates are transmitted to our safety response team via a secure encrypted connection.
  • The coordinates are used solely to dispatch appropriate emergency assistance — including police (119), NCPA (1929), or our field response team.
  • Location data is not stored permanently. Once the emergency is resolved or contact is established, location data is deleted.
  • Location data is never used for any purpose other than the emergency response for which it was shared.
  • For children (JusticeKids): the SOS system allows a child to call for help without identifying themselves by name or NIC.
Important — SOS Limitations
The SOS system contacts our safety response team who will then contact appropriate authorities. It is not a substitute for directly calling Police 119, NCPA 1929 (child protection), or Ambulance 1990 in an immediate life-threatening emergency. Always call emergency services directly if you can do so safely.

Section 8

Data Storage & Security

Personal data collected through the Justice LK Ecosystem is stored using the following infrastructure:

  • Cloudflare Workers & KV — Case data, consultation requests, and document analysis results are processed and stored via Cloudflare's edge computing infrastructure. Cloudflare provides enterprise-grade encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest.
  • Google Sheets (JusticeLK routing) — Case summaries routed to the Legal Aid Commission may be transmitted via secure webhook to a Google Sheets instance controlled by the Justice LK team. Access is restricted to authorised field officers and attorneys.
  • GitHub Pages (static hosting) — Platform code is served via GitHub Pages. No personal data is stored on GitHub Pages infrastructure.
  • Browser-only (JusticeKids, quiz data) — All JusticeKids quiz data, certificate generation, and session-only chat data is processed entirely in the user's browser and is not transmitted to any server.

We implement the following security measures:

  • All data transmission uses HTTPS with TLS 1.2 or higher encryption.
  • Case management data (JusticeLK) is accessible only to authenticated field officers using username/password credentials.
  • Document uploads are stored in encrypted format and are accessible only to the assigned attorney or Legal Aid Commission officer.
  • SOS location data is transmitted via encrypted channel and is not logged to permanent storage.
  • Regular security reviews of platform code and infrastructure.

Section 10

Data Sharing & Disclosure

We share personal data only in the following circumstances:

  • Legal Aid Commission of Sri Lanka — Case files registered through JusticeLK where the user has been assessed as eligible for government legal aid. Data is shared as part of the core service.
  • Registered Private Attorneys (LexLK network) — Case files, document analysis results, and consultation request details are shared with the attorney selected by the user, or matched by the platform, with the user's knowledge and consent.
  • Emergency Services — SOS location data is shared with appropriate emergency services (Police 119, NCPA 1929, Ambulance 1990) when a user activates the SOS button.
  • Legal Obligation — We will disclose personal data where required by a court order, regulatory authority, or other lawful demand under Sri Lankan law. We will notify the affected user where legally permitted to do so.
  • Institutional Partners (aggregated only) — Aggregated, non-identifying statistics (total cases registered, districts served, case categories) may be shared with partner organisations (Ministry of Justice, Legal Aid Commission, UNDP, UNICEF) for programme reporting purposes.

We will never sell, rent, or trade personal data to any third party for commercial purposes.
